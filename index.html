Siamese Revolution of 1932

Early morning of June 24, 1932, Khana Ratsadon (People's Party) launched a revolution to end absolute monarchy in Siam, today known as Thailand. The civilian-military political party introduced democracy and consitution to the nation. The Siamese Revolution of 1932 set a stage for the power struggle between civilian, the military, and the palace, which is still ongoing almost nine decades later.

The revolution is a watershed moment in the history of Thailand and its democracy, and the anti-democratic ruling faction of today is actively erasing its legacy. The brass plaque commemorating the event, embedded in a street of the Royal Plaza where the leader of Khana Ratsadon read its first declaration, was removed overnight. A pro-monarchy plaque was left in its place. Statues and monuments for the revolution and Khana Ratsadon have been removed or destroyed, most notably Laksi Monument erected in 1936 to commemorate soldiers who died fighting against the royalist rebellion in 1933.
